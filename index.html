The Daily Dozen

Questions to ask when creating your Daily RPM

💡 Step 1 — Capture: Ideas, Wants & Needs
1
Brainstorm: What needs to get done today? What are any and all the outcomes, actions, projects, and communications that need to happen today?
🎯 Step 2 — Create Your RPM Plan
2
Result / Outcome: What are the most important outcomes/results that I need to produce today?
3
Purpose: Why do I want to do this? To support who or what? What's my real purpose?
4
MAP — Massive Action Plan: What specific actions must I take in order to achieve this result or this outcome?
5
Musts: Which of these actions are absolute musts for me? Asterisk those 20% that make 80% of the difference.
6
Duration: How long will each of these items take? Then, add these times up to find out the duration of the entire RPM Block.
7
Leverage: Which of these actions could I leverage to someone else? Ask yourself: "How can I leverage this result? What other resources do I have available — an assistant, outsourcing, trades, technology, etc.?" Some actions can be accomplished without using your own time and brainpower. Mark these with an L in the Lev column.
8
Create other RPM Blocks: What other result or outcome is extremely important for me to achieve today? Repeat questions 2–8 until you have created an RPM Block for each of your outcomes/results.
📅 Step 3 — Commit & Schedule
9
When do I want to commit my time to do this? How many hours (or minutes) do I want to commit? What is the block time that I will commit for it?
10
When is the exact time that I will work on this RPM Block or action item? First, schedule those items that must happen at a specific time. Then, schedule blocks of time to work on outcomes. Remember, you must schedule on your daily planner.
🎉 Step 4 — Complete & Achieve: Celebrate!
11
What did I accomplish today? Did I achieve my most important outcomes? What roles did I fulfill? What are some Magic Moments I want to remember or celebrate? Cross off items that are done, and carry over any items that still need to be done to the next day's capture section.
12
What did I learn today?
